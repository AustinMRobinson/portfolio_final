About Me

When did all of this start?

I started designing 7 years ago because my best friend and I wanted to make a YouTube channel for our gaming clan. We used to ball out of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2, and we made quite a few montages back in our day. In order for our clan "KhaotiiK Gaming" to look legit, I needed to make us a logo, channel art, and our videos.

To do this, I taught myself design and editing by watching YouTube tutorial videos. I'd do this in my free time away from school, sports, and friends.. It quickly became an obsession, and I continued to do it throughout high school. I designed whenever I could, and used various outlets like student government campaigns to practice my skills. The big turning point for me in high school was when I joined a class specifically surrounding sports media. Designing advertisements and posters for our sports teams was an absolute blast, and I really started to get good at it. This momentum followed me to college at Texas A&M.. And that's where the real growth began.

The Volta

When I got to A&M, I sought out any and every opportunity to design. First, working in the Marketing department at A&M, and designing fun t-shirts for my fraternity. Then, in the spring of my sophomore year I joined The Owner's List. This is when I started to make real money for my very real skills. Designing logo, advertisements, user interfaces, everything. It was a trial by fire in many ways, and it only compounded my growth. In the year and a half since joining and eventually leaving The Owner's List, I have seen a huge uptake in my skills in every category.

I fell in love with web and app (UI) design. Then, more recently, I taught myself front-end web development so I could finally make a website from start to finish - design and code. It killed me to hand off my designs and not control exactly how they turned out, and I also felt inadequate in the fact that the developers had a skill I didn't have. So over the month of December 2016, I became a unicorn.

What is a unicorn?

A unicorn is a designer who codes. They are pretty rare, hence the name "unicorn". Being a unicorn is not my end goal, I want to continue to grow as a designer and to learn much more than front-end development.

What's next?

I am still only a junior in college, so I plan on teaching myself as much as I can before I graduate - in order to have as many options as possible. The next skill I plan on acquiring is a toss up between learning back-end web development and app development. We'll see. My main goal is to be the best T-shaped creative I can be.

What is a T-Shaped Creative?

A T-shaped creative is my own twist on what a T-shaped designer is.. Which is a simple concept explained by the capital letter "T".

The top of the T is general knowledge, and the lower you get, the more specialized the skills and knowledge become. So the bottom of the T is my "UI Design" because it's my strongest skill.

A T-shaped creative is someone who is able to be multifaceted in their creative skills. Someone who has general knowledge of a lot of areas, but then hones in their skills to specialize in 1-3 areas.

My areas of general knowledge are: videography/editing, advertisement design, print design, and photography.

My three areas of specialization are:
Branding
UI Design
Development

The purpose of being a good T-shaped creative is to be able to collaborate well and to provide most any solution a client needs.

Collaboration is extremely important because I know that I will always need to work alongside other people for the rest of my life. Being able to understand what everyone's role is very important to me, because I believe intelligent communication is dependent on depth of subject knowledge.

Providing for a client is obviously important because half of the reason I do what I do is to see the look on someone's face when you present them something that they are blown away by. So, if I am skilled in many areas, I can do most anything they want - and do it well.

21 year old creative from Denver, Colorado in school at Texas A&M - studying MIS. What's after that? Who knows!
