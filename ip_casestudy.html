The annual event for my fraternity, nailing a new look and web presence was important.

00

Introduction

Even though this is a philanthropy event that draws a similar crowd every year, I wanted to do my best in order to lay a quality foundation for the brand for years to come.

01

The Brief

This project was brought to me by the new 2017 Island Party committee (I did the logo for the 2016 event).

Since this was volunteer work, the committee pegged me as the best man for the job, and I'm glad they did.

The main priority was to create a new logo and website that would transcend the standards that Texas A&M students had become accustomed to.

02

Research & Planning

Island Party is a brand that changes their logo every year, so having that a luxury was nice. However, this year's committee had a very specific vision for the brand.

This was probably my worst client interaction I've ever had, and I handled it poorly.

With that said, this was one of the best projects for teaching me how to handle myself with clients, and that's why I'm explaining it.

The audience for this is college students, and potential sponsors. The sponsors need to see a quality brand to attach their name to, and students want a good website that is mobile-friendly.

05

Coding the Website

My newest skill, front-end development might be my favorite thing I've ever learned. Finally being able to bring my designs to life is so awesome.

This was the second fully custom hand-coded site that I ever did, and it worked out well. It took me 9 days from beginning to end, another crazy deadline.

06

I grew as a person

Working with friends can go one of two ways: really good or really bad. In this case, it went the bad way. Overcoming the bad experience on both sides was difficult, but important.

The reason it didn't go well is that I had a very refined, singular focus, and the client did as well. Our ideas had aligned, it would've been an easier process. However, they did not align. At all. We were both very unwilling to give ground on each side, and my pride was ultimately the main issue.

This was the biggest reality check for me because I was unable to swallow my pride and realize that the client was most important in this situation. So once I was able to do that, each side was able to sacrifice here and there, and we came up with an end product that we were happy with.
