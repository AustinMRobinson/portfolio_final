Introduction

Running for Yell Leader as a regular student (meaning a student not in the Corps of Cadets) is no small task. Nailing this was important.

01

The Brief

When Cason approached me with the prospect of running for junior yell, I knew it was going to be an uphill battle. And it certainly has been.

I accepted the offer without hesitation, mostly because I wanted to help a friend out. The past campaigns for yell leader were lacking in many ways, and no one had designed a quality brand or a fully custom website. I wanted to do both of those things.

One of the biggest things that Cason wanted was a versatile logo and a good website. I was up for the challenge.

02

Research & Planning

I had the opportunity to work on Steven Lanz's successful senior yell campaign design during my freshman year, and I had a good idea of what it took to make something that fit the tone.

This was one of those times that everything just fell together perfectly.

Cason and the team gave me full creative control, and they had a general idea of what they wanted. However, in reality, they were going to be happy with whatever I produced.

The audience is 100% made up of college students, so a brand and website that appealed to them (and made them want to vote) was important. Making it easy to vote and learn about Cason was also important.

Coding the Website

My newest skill, front-end development might be my favorite thing I've ever learned. Finally being able to bring my designs to life is so awesome.

This was the first fully custom hand-coded site that I ever did, and it was quite a success. Designed and launched in 6 (!!!) days, this trial by fire definitely paid off.

06

I grew as a friend

Working with friends can go one of two ways: really good or really bad. In this case, it went the really really good way. I made something that everyone was happy with.

I learned my lesson from the Island Party mishap and we were all happy with the outcome.

Being able to play such a huge role in a large part of my friend's life was so important to me. I felt like I was finally able to use my skills to really help out a buddy, and it was a great feeling.
