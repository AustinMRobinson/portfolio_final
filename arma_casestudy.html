Introduction

The task of redesigning the Arma Centrum website was a tall order. It was my first real site that I would design and have developed. My knowledge of handoff standards and developer expectations were abysmal.

Nonetheless, we accepted the challenge and went for it.

01

The Brief

Jay, one of the co-owners of Arma approached me over the summer of 2016 and offered me the job of designing their new website. I would be working alongside him and a friend of ours, Nick Teters, for the project.

Arma’s goal was to create the best apparel site in the Gaming industry. They wanted to stand head and shoulders above the rest with a website that matched their killer brand (designed by MDC).

02

Research & Planning

It’s important to understand just how young competitive gaming is. There are not many conventions that are currently established for this new space, so it is important to stick to the few things we know to be true.

The audience is going to be more tech-savvy than the average user.

These gamers have been using UI’s for a long time, and they don’t need their hand held through the process. They want information about the product as quickly as possible.

Giving this audience exactly what they wanted in as few clicks as possible was our main goal in order to drive sales for Arma.

The most important part of the design process. Wireframing, regardless of the fidelity level, is integral in the development of the user experience. Having a user prototype your design at it’s most basic point allows flaws in your structure to show through.

I learned. A lot.

For your thumb’s sake.. I’ll keep this short. This was by far my most valuable project I have ever embarked on. Not in monetary form. 100% from a learning and growth perspective. Working side by side with talented designers and Nick was very beneficial and I learned so much about what it takes to design and handoff a full fledged website. This website is the point that I fell in love with UI design, and it gave me the itch to learn web development.
