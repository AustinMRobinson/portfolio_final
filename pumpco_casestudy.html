Pumpco Inc. Branding

My largest client and one of my proudest moments as a designer. A new visual identity for Pumpco.

00

Introduction

Pumpco is a very large, very valuable company. They boast the largest pipeline construction business in the United States. Rebranding a company of this size was a very slow and calculated process.

01

The Brief

This project took place during my time at The Owner's List. My boss, Doak, approached me with the proposition to rebrand Pumpco.

Of course I said yes. I would be crazy to turn down a project of this size, and I knew it would be a very valuable learning experience to work alongside such an established name. This was the project that gave me great confidence in my abilities. And it made my parents proud of me, so that was cool.

The main priority was to create a logo that would last decades due to it's simplicity and timelessness.

02

Research & Planning

Pumpco is an old brand, and their branding matched that. Our goal was to bring them into the 21st century with their new branding.

Rebranding a company with old(er) management is never easy. Tradition is important.

Overcoming the obstacle of being too entrenched in tradition was also important. We had a lot of back and forth with the management at Pumpco, and they were very receptive to go in an updated direction.

The audience for the oil and gas space is a much different group than I am used to. I looked at other brands in the sector for weeks trying to determine what the right direction was going to be.

03

Brand development

Sketching

The most important part of the design process. Sketching is necessary in order to be able to iterate and explore quickly and with the perfect amount of time and effectiveness.

Design

Once there are a few sketches that the client likes, I move forward to the design process - fire up Illustrator.

04

I grew as a designer

With a client of this size, and so much on the line, I would be remiss not to mention my growth. This was my first real job as a designer, and what I consider to be the turning point in my journey as a designer.

Being able to tell my parents I designed a brand for a company worth hundreds of millions of dollars was a big, big day.
