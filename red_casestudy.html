Red Reserve Website Redesign

As part of a recruitment challenge, I attempted to create a revolutionary site for eSports.

00

Introduction

This task was definitely more difficult than I expected, and I found that variation was the hardest part of the whole process.

I tried a million combinations to get the right formula, but I think the final product was worth it.

01

The Brief

For this project, I had no constraints. The purpose was to pitch a concept design to the owners of the company in hopes that they would like it enough to put it into production.

We got very close to that goal. Unfortunately, even though the owners absolutely loved the design , it didn't end up working out from a logistics standpoint. However, coming up with a design for them that fit their needs exactly was extremely important.

02

Research & Planning

Just like with Arma, it’s important to understand just how young competitive gaming is. There are not many conventions that are currently established for this new space, so it is important to stick to the few things we know to be true.

The audience is going to be more tech-savvy than the average user.

These gamers have been using UI’s for a long time, and they don’t need their hand held through the process. They want information about the product as quickly as possible.

03

UI Development

Wireframing

The most important part of the design process. Wireframing, regardless of the fidelity level, is integral in the development of the user experience. Having a user prototype your design at it’s most basic point allows flaws in your structure to show through.

Design

Once a good foundation is laid, and the wireframes pass the user testing - it is time to design.

04

I learned a thing or two.

This was my first full-fledged web design. And to say it was a smashing success would definitely be an understatement. This website, by far, was my best-received work ever. From this point on, I knew web design was what I wanted to do, and this project helped me build connections to land my work at Arma.

I am very happy with how this website turned out, and my goal is to learn enough back-end development to finally bring this bad boy to life.
