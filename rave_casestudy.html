BYX Rave Branding

My freshman year of college I did a logo for our annual rave. It was our biggest one ever. Now, as a Junior, I did it again.

00

Introduction

With Rave being BYX's annual event, it switches brands every single year. With this me being my second time to design a brand for Rave, I had a general idea of what look would suit the brand best.

01

The Brief

Like I said before, I have rebranded Rave in the past. Freshman year the theme was also 'Red, White and Blue', so I had the experience that the committee felt was necessary.

I said yes, mostly because the brand I designed in 2014 was severely lacking compared to what my skills looked like in 2016. I felt bad about where I had left it, so I was delighted to revisit it and give it another shot.

The main priority was to create a logo that would be easily identifiable with the general public at Texas A&M and that scaled well.

02

Research & Planning

Coming up with an identity for Rave was surpisingly pretty easy. Having the barn was extremely important because that is the only consistent thing about the event is that it is held at the barn every year.

Rave 2016 was a very fun brand to work with - I was able to see my progress over the course of 2 years

My only constraints that I was given is the colors. I had to use red, white, and blue.. which I was more than happy to do. Coming up with a recognizable brand meant a great use of colors and a strong typeface.

03

Sketching

The most important part of the design process. Sketching is necessary in order to be able to iterate and explore quickly and with the perfect amount of time and effectiveness.

Design

04

I learned a bit

Overall, this was a fun project, but not one that tested or challenged me in many ways. I am very happy with the outcome, and the client (one of my best friends) was too.
